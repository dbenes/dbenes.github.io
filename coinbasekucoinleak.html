  • Data of 3.5 million US cryptocurrency exchange users exposed in leak

  • (17 June 2022)
  •  
  • The private details of 3.5 million US cryptocurrency exchange users has gone up for sale on a Chinese darkweb forum. The leak includes data stolen from "Coinbase, Kucoin and others" over recent months.
  •  
  • Similar to other leaks of cryptocurrenct exchanges, the data includes the full names, addresses, phone numbers, emails and dates of birth of registered users. The data is contained in an Microsoft excel spreadsheet and does not appear to include passwords or financial information.
  •  
  • The hackers are charging $700 USD in Bitcoin for access to the data, which they say will be provided via link to an MEGA.io online storage account. Sales records indicate the advertisement went live on 8 May 2022.
  •  
  • The hacker's "sample list" of identities, seen by Niutoh, includes a serving US police officer, a tax accountant and a publishing company manager.
