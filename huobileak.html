  • Data from 200,000 UK Huobi customers exposed in leak

  • (27 October 2022)
  •  
  • The private details of 200,000 UK users of the Huobi cryptocurrency platform has been leaked on the chinese darkweb marketplace. The hackers are asking for $350 for access to the spreadsheet of stolen user data.
  •  
  • Huobi, a Seychelles based cryptocurrency exchange originally founded in China, has not yet responded to the news of the leak. The company claims to serve +10 million customers across the world, and is ranked as the 9th largest Cryptocurrency exchange by the comparison website CoinMarketCap.
  •  
  • The trove of private data includes the names, mobile numbers and home cities of registered UK-based customers, as well as their wallet values in Tether (USDT). The information is dated as 20 October 2022.
  •  
  • Private keys seem to be unaffected by the breach. A "sample list" of breached data can be seen below, which has been censored by Niutoh as it includes unredacted customer names and mobile phone numbers.
